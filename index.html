Key Takeaway

The current "Knowledge Transfer" sector (3 million) represents the intergenerational engine. Its non-recognition creates a terminal risk of "Epistemicide."

Formalizing these practitioners could provide 1.5 million health workers into the primary care network immediately.

2. Dimensions of Marginalization

Misappropriation & Biopiracy

Corporate capitalization on resources like traditional seeds and medicinal plants without fair compensation. Treating IKS as "public domain" results in billions of Rands in uncaptured IP value.

Systemic Erasure

Force-fitting IKS into Western qualification frameworks (NQF) strips knowledge of its spiritual and community validation, effectively "killing" the system while trying to certify it.

Stagnant Solutions

Ignoring unique epistemologies in astronomy, environmental management, and metallurgy prevents the development of localized, sustainable technology for modern crises.
